Why History Matters

History helps us understand how people lived, built societies, solved problems, and changed the world. Every era leaves lessons that shape our future.

Basic World History Timeline

  • c. 3000 BCE: Early civilizations emerge in Mesopotamia and Egypt.
  • c. 500 BCE: Classical age expands in Greece, India, and China.
  • 476 CE: Western Roman Empire falls, beginning the Medieval period in Europe.
  • 1492: Age of Exploration accelerates global contact.
  • 1760–1840: Industrial Revolution transforms production and daily life.
  • 1914–1945: Two world wars reshape modern international order.

Major Civilizations

Mesopotamia

Often called the cradle of civilization, known for cuneiform writing, city-states, and law codes.

Ancient China

Innovations in paper, silk, philosophy, and governance deeply influenced East Asian history.

Rome

Rome shaped law, engineering, architecture, and administrative systems still studied today.

Historical Figures

Cleopatra VII

The final active ruler of Ptolemaic Egypt and one of the most recognized leaders of the ancient world.

Albert Einstein

A physicist whose theory of relativity transformed scientific understanding in the 20th century.

Mahatma Gandhi

A key leader of India’s independence movement, known for nonviolent resistance and civil action.
